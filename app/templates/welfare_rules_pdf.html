Contribution Rules

Monthly contributions system with penalties and benefits calculation.

Important Notice

Those who are eligible to receive the welfare money are only those who contribute; therefore, those who do not contribute are not eligible to receive the money.

Contribution Guidelines

1. Late Payment Penalties

5% penalty per month for late contributions. Multiple months accumulate penalties progressively.

Example: 2 months late = 10% penalty, 3 months late = 15% penalty

2. Back Payments

Missed months must be paid for the specific period they were missed. Payments are applied to the oldest outstanding month first.

3. Benefits Calculation

Number of times you can receive help = Number of months you've contributed.

Example: 3 months contribution = eligible for support 3 times

4. Partial Contributions

For those contributing irregularly ("mwa apo ndi apo"), penalties apply based on the number of months skipped.

Penalty Calculation Guide

Understanding how late payment penalties are calculated:

Penalty Rate: 5% per month

Formula: Penalty = Monthly Contribution × (5% × Number of Late Months)

Example Calculation:
Monthly Contribution: K1,000
2 months late: K1,000 × (5% × 2) = K1,000 × 10% = K100 penalty
Total to pay: K1,000 + K100 = K1,100
3 months late:
K1,000 × (5% × 3) = K1,000 × 15% = K150 penalty
Total to pay: K1,000 + K150 = K1,150

Benefits Eligibility Guide

How your contributions determine your support eligibility:

1:1 Benefit Ratio

Rule: Each month of contribution = One time eligible for support

Scenario 1:
6 months contributed = Eligible for support 6 times total
(Can be any combination of funeral, sickness, wedding, or member death support)
Scenario 2:
2 months contributed = Eligible for support 2 times only
Once used, must contribute more to be eligible again
Scenario 3:
Member death support counts as one support instance
If a member contributed for 5 months and passes away:
- Family receives K80,000 support
- This counts as 1 support instance used
- Member had contributed for 4 other potential support instances
